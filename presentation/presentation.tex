\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Conclusion}
%\frametitle{Abstract}
%TODO: Past tense
We implemented in Gallina a hierarchy of functions that calculate
the upper inverses to the hyperoperation/Ackermann hierarchy.

\bigskip

Our functions run in $\Theta(b)$ for inputs expressed
in unary, and $\bigO(b^2)$ for inputs expressed in binary ($b$ = bitlength).

\bigskip

We used our inverses to define linear-time functions---$\Theta(b)$ for both unary- 
and binary-represented inputs---that compute the upper inverse of the 
diagonal Ackermann %\linebreak[3]
function~$\Ack(n)$. 

\bigskip

We showed that these functions are consistent with the usual definition of the inverse Ackermann function~$\alpha(n)$.
\keywords{Ackermann  \and hyperoperations \and Coq}
\end{frame}
